Playground.
01
Ulises Carrión - Mail Art and the Big Monster from Second Thoughts
The reading emphasized that Mail Art transcends the mere use of the postal system that is, it is not just a medium but a support, similar to other traditional art forms. The reading critically examined the misconceptions about Mail Art, stated that it's beyond just creating postcard-like art. It also discussed the limitations and rules imposed by the postal system, framing it as a "Big Monster" and positioning Mail Art as a form of resistance.
02
Laurel Schwulst & Édouard U - Selections from Creative Independent and Arena How Do You Use the Internet Mindfully
The reading explored the internet's potential for creativity and knowledge-building, emphasizing the importance of personal websites over social media platforms controlled by corporations. The author stated that personal websites allow for a more authentic expression and control over one's digital presence, challenging viewers to think of the internet as a collaborative space that can reflect individuality. The author also suggested that by creating personal websites, individuals can contribute to a more diverse and human-centric web.
03
Olia Lialina - STILL THERE Ruins and Templates of Geocities from Lost and Living (in) Archives.
The reading discusses the impact of Geocities shutdown and preservation efforts, highlighting the significance of Geocities as a cultural and digital artifact of early web communities. The author explores how Geocities served as an entry point to the web for many, embodying the amateur spirit of early Internet culture through its user-created pages and unique neighborhoods. The rescue and archival efforts post-shutdown preserved a significant portion of Geocities. The reading reflects emphasizing its role in shaping online identities and communities.
04
Bojana Coklyat _ Shannon Finnegan - Selections from Alt-Text as Poetry Workbook
The text explored the creative potential of alt-text, traditionally seen as a tool for web accessibility. The authors argued for a poetic approach to alt-text, encouraging readers to think beyond mere description to engage with the expressive possibilities of language. This approach not only enhances accessibility but also enriches the experience for all web users, making the internet a more inclusive and engaging community.
05
Ramsey Nasser - A Personal Computer for Children of All Cultures from Decolonising the Digital
The text critically examines the linguistic and cultural biases inherent in the field of computing, particularly through the lens of the One Laptop Per Child project. By sharing his experience in creating Qalb, he further illustrates the significant challenges in decolonizing digital technology. His work underscores the importance of addressing these biases to truly make technology inclusive and accessible to all cultures, highlighting a broader issue of linguistic and cultural dominance in the development and implementation of global technologies.
06
Ben Duvall - Selections from New Modernism(s)
The text explores the evolution of graphic design, identifying a shift towards "hypermodernism." This new phase reflects the profound impact of digital technology on how we create and interpret signs in design. Duvall illustrates this through the changing nature of surfaces—from physical to digital—and signs, emphasizing the complex, interconnected meanings they now carry. The text invites us to consider the broader implications of these changes in design on society and individual identity, and how designers can engage with current social and political issues.
07
Dot Tuer - Beyond the New Media Frame The Poetics of Absence in Vera Frenkel’s String Games
Dot Tuer's article delves into Vera Frenkel’s creative exploration of memory and absence, particularly through "String Games," a pioneering teleconferencing performance. It shows how Frenkel used technology to question how we connect and remember in a digital age. Tuer points out that Frenkel’s work, from "String Games" to "Body Missing," consistently probes the gaps technology creates in human interaction. The discussion focuses on how these artworks offer a critical perspective on digital interactions and their impact on our sense of historical and personal connection.
08
Paul Soulellis - Performing the Feed
The text dives into how the ways we share and consume information have changed, moving from traditional methods like books and posters to digital streams and feeds that are endless and ever-changing. Through his exploration, Soulellis highlights the shift from the act of posting—rooted in physicality and visibility—to the perpetual motion of the digital feed. The author points out this shift affects not just how we get our news or entertainment but also challenges our understanding of what it means to publish and be part of a shared, online public space.
09
Neta Bomani & Sabii Borno - Beyond Dark Matter from Logic Issue 15 Beacons
The text explores the complexities of technology through the story of Gem. The story delves into the societal and personal impacts of computing, addressing issues of control, history, and the potential for technology to both connect and isolate. The story critiques the inherent power dynamics in technological language and systems, and highlights the importance of community and human connections beyond digital interfaces.
10
Boris Groys - Art on the Internet
The text explores the shifts that art and literature have experienced in their transition from traditional cultural institutions to the Internet. The author discusses how the Internet, as a less selective medium, challenges previous notions of artistic and literary selection, which often seemed arbitrary or influenced by institutional power dynamics. Art and literature become "de-fictionalized" due to their integration into the broader context of real-world information. This shift alters how artworks are perceived and blurs the lines between fiction and reality, potentially leading to a greater democratization of cultural production.
